Defining a precise process to assemble rugged servers for the military, machine
learning, or other computing systems through automated tooling — making each
phase seamlessly easy to follow for all personnel involved in the work.

## Reason

Assembling complex rugged servers from scratch requires coordinating across
multiple teams to achieve high-quality, efficient results. Without a clear plan
and design, the process can become stressful for the teams and — what is worse
— result in low quality and poor efficiency.

## First and Foremost

The complex process of assembling a rugged server must be divided into small,
independent sub-processes that are interconnected phase by phase to achieve the
final result.

## Tools

The entire process can be communicated through different technologies — from
something as simple as Excel or Word documentation for small companies, to more
advanced online platforms for large-scale assembly projects — so that technicians
can be shown, in simple and fast terms, how to put things together in whichever
sub-process they are working on.

## Results

Delivering high-quality rugged servers with 3.4 defects per million
opportunities — what is known as Six Sigma — in a fast-paced environment.
